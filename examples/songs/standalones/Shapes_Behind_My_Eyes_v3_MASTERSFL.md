## Meter Design (Step 4b)

```
Intro: Free (whispered, no meter — breath and space)
Verse 1: Iambic Tetrameter (da-DUM da-DUM da-DUM da-DUM — conversational, intimate, natural speech)
Pre-Chorus: Anapestic Trimeter (da-da-DUM da-da-DUM da-da-DUM — forward momentum, building)
Chorus: Trochaic Tetrameter (DUM-da DUM-da DUM-da DUM-da — buoyant, slightly melancholic, hook-friendly)
Verse 2: Iambic Tetrameter (same as V1 — grounding, intimate)
Bridge: Amphibrach Trimeter (da-DUM-da da-DUM-da da-DUM-da — flowing, balanced, reflective CONTRAST)
Final Chorus: Trochaic Tetrameter + Spondee endings (DUM-da DUM-da DUM-DUM — buoyant then emphatic resolution)
Outro: Pyrrhic (da-da da-da — dissolving, weightless, fading)
```

**Reasoning:** The verse needs to feel like intimate conversation (iambic = natural English speech). Pre-chorus needs forward motion to LEAN into the chorus (anapestic = galloping forward). Chorus needs to feel slightly floating/melancholic but memorable (trochaic = lighter, buoyant with a falling quality). Bridge is the TURN — needs to feel different from everything before (amphibrach = flowing balance, gentler rhythm). Final chorus adds spondee weight at line endings for resolution/acceptance.
