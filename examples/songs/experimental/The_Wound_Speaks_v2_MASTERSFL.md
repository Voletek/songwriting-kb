## Meter Design (Step 4b)

```
Intro: No meter (instrumental drone — 30s silence)
Movement I: Pyrrhic Dimeter → Spondee Monometer (2 weightless syllables then 2 heavy — like breathing then cracking)
  Pattern: da-da da-da | DUM-DUM (flow, flow, then WEIGHT)
Movement II: Iambic Trimeter (slightly more structured — the Rift is gathering strength to speak)
The Plea: Mixed — starts Pyrrhic (exhausted, weightless), ends Spondee (emphatic, desperate)
Final Movement: Dactylic Trimeter (formal, grandiose, ancient — the Rift speaks with full authority)
Outro: Pyrrhic dissolve (back to weightless)
```

**Reasoning:** The Rift is a non-human entity that speaks with effort. Pyrrhic (unstressed-unstressed) = weightless, like air escaping a wound — minimal rhythmic pressure. Spondee moments = the weight of pain landing. The progression from pyrrhic to dactylic mirrors the Rift gathering enough strength to fully speak — from gasping whispers to ancient formal proclamation. This should steer Suno toward: minimal rhythmic emphasis in early sections (matching the drone/ambient style), building toward more structured delivery in the Final Movement.

## MasterofSFL Techniques Applied — Changelog

1. **Italian tempo marking:** "Grave" in Style Prompt + `[Tempo: Grave]` at top of lyrics. Plus `[sostenuto]` and `[ritardando]` for mid-song tempo feel shifts.
2. **Chord progression tags:** Before every section — Bb pedal, i-bII oscillation, i-bVI-bVII-i buildup
3. **Full sandwiching:** Every section has `[Mood:]` + `[Instrument:]` + `[Chord Progression:]` BEFORE structure tag, `[Vocals:]` AFTER
4. **Aggressive blank lines:** 2-3 blank lines between ALL phrases — forces the 60% silence that this song demands. Each blank line = ~2.5s of breathing room at this tempo.
5. **Updated sliders:** From 65-70%/55-60% to 75%/95% (Shock Test) — maximum experimental + maximum style lock
6. **Meter design:** Pyrrhic dominant (matching drone/ambient) with Spondee punctuation (weight moments) and Dactylic authority (Final Movement)
7. **Italian tempo transitions:** `[sostenuto]` before Movement II (sustained, heavy), `[ritardando]` before outro (broadening, slowing into dissolution)
8. **Return tag:** `[Return to main song]` after instrumental intro — signals vocal entry point clearly

## Expected Differences from v1

If MasterofSFL's claims hold, v2 should produce:
- **Stronger drone commitment** (95% Style Influence should lock the unusual genre request — less chance of Suno adding unwanted drums/structure)
- **Better tempo control** (Grave + `[Tempo: Grave]` reinforcement should keep it funereal instead of drifting toward standard ballad pace)
- **More harmonic precision** (chord tags specifying Bb pedal and Phrygian movement should guide the harmonic content more reliably)
- **Clearer section transitions** (sandwiching gives each section its own identity — especially the dramatic mood shift for The Plea)
- **More silence/space** (aggressive blank lines + pyrrhic meter = minimal rhythmic pressure + physical gaps)
- **Smoother post-intro entry** (return tag after 30s instrumental should help Suno transition to vocal cleanly)

**Most critical test:** Does 95% Style Influence actually LOCK this unusual genre combination (drone + sacred + Icelandic post-classical) where 55-60% might have let Suno "normalize" it toward generic ambient?
