## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Overall Verdict:** The SOP is well-structured and comprehensive. A motivated new user CAN follow it to completion. However, there are several friction points that would slow down or confuse someone working without the existing Fractured Shadows Bible as a reference. The biggest gap: the SOP assumes you'll look at an example, but never explicitly tells you one exists or where to find it.

**Completion Status:** All 12 steps completed. Album Bible, steering file, and hook file produced.

## OVERALL ASSESSMENT

### What Worked Well

1. **The phased structure is logical** — Concept → Registry → Rules → Motifs → Documentation flows naturally.
2. **Step 6 (Hard Rules) is outstanding** — The category table with examples is the gold standard for SOP instruction.
3. **The "Rule:" callouts** (like "sonic palette should MIRROR emotional arc") give rationale that helps you make decisions, not just follow instructions.
4. **Step 9 (Callbacks)** forces resolution-thinking, not just reference-thinking. Excellent.
5. **The "What NOT to Do" table** at the end is genuinely useful and prevents common mistakes.
6. **Time estimates** are realistic and helpful for planning.

### What Was Confusing

1. **Step 10 is the weakest step** — it asks you to assemble something from pieces without showing what "assembled" looks like. The gap between "here are 11 sections" and "here's a finished bible" is the biggest leap in the SOP.
2. **"Versioning Opportunities"** appears from nowhere in Step 10, undefined.
3. **The SOP 05 cross-reference** in Step 10 is a speed bump — you must leave this SOP, read another, extract content, and return. No guidance on what to extract.
4. **Step 12's hooks** assume knowledge of a system that's never introduced.
5. **Step 4 vs "What NOT to Do"** creates ambiguity: fill in all keys now, or leave room for discovery?

### What's Missing

1. **"See existing example at [path]"** — This single line, added to Step 10, would save a new user 30+ minutes of confusion. The Fractured Shadows Bible is the Rosetta Stone for this SOP, and it's never mentioned.
2. **Format specification for the bible** — Markdown heading levels, section order, how much detail per section.
3. **Scaling guidance** — The SOP is written for ~12-17 track albums. An 8-track album needs slightly different proportions (motif introduction window, number of phases, etc.). A brief "Scaling for shorter/longer albums" section would help.
4. **Multi-album repo guidance** — If the repo already has a concept album (Fractured Shadows), how does mine coexist? Do steering files conflict? This isn't addressed.
5. **Definition of "Versioning Opportunities"** — needs to be a sub-step or at minimum a one-line explanation.
6. **Hook mechanism explanation** — What does creating a hook file ACTUALLY do? Is it automated? Manual? A checklist?
7. **Music theory dependency** — Step 5 assumes knowledge of key relationships, modes, and intervals. A pointer to `MUSIC_PRODUCTION_THEORY.md` or similar would help less theory-literate users.

### What Would Trip Up a Real New User

| Trap | Likely Outcome |
|---|---|
| No example bible referenced | User creates a file with wrong structure/detail level |
| "Versioning Opportunities" undefined | User skips it or invents something wrong |
| SOP 05 reference with no extraction guidance | User either skips it or copies ALL of SOP 05 into their bible |
| Step 4 keys: plan now or later? | User freezes trying to decide all keys upfront |
| Step 12 hooks: no mechanism explained | User creates a file that does nothing and doesn't know it |
| Steering file naming convention unclear | User creates file with wrong name, Kiro doesn't load it |
| Non-vocal characters don't fit voice template | User forces a vocal template onto non-vocal elements, gets confused |

## RECOMMENDATIONS

### Priority 1 (Would eliminate most confusion)
1. Add to Step 10: "See `references/FRACTURED_SHADOWS_BIBLE.md` for a completed example of an album bible."
2. Add a sub-step or footnote defining "Versioning Opportunities" — one sentence and one example.
3. For the SOP 05 reference, add: "Include a 4-6 line Extension Protocol section. See SOP 05 Step 2-3 for what to include, or copy the protocol from the example bible."

### Priority 2 (Would improve flow)
4. Add scaling notes: "For albums under 10 tracks, use 3 emotional phases and introduce motifs in the first 2-3 tracks."
5. In Step 4, resolve the tension: "Fill in keys where you're certain. Mark 'TBD' where you want flexibility. Aim for at least 60% decided before moving on."
6. In Step 7, add: "For non-vocal presences (ambient entities, environmental forces), adapt this template to describe sonic manifestation rather than vocal performance."

### Priority 3 (Nice to have)
7. Brief hook mechanism explanation in Step 12: "Hook files are checklists that [Kiro/you] reference when reviewing new song drafts. They don't auto-enforce — they provide the criteria to check against."
8. Multi-album guidance: "If your repo contains other albums, create your files alongside them. Steering files are album-specific and won't conflict."
9. Link to `MUSIC_PRODUCTION_THEORY.md` in Step 5 for theory-light users.

---

## FINAL VERDICT

**SOP 04 is a solid B+.** The creative guidance is genuinely excellent — Steps 1-3 and 6-9 are well-written, well-reasoned, and produce good output. The documentation assembly phase (Steps 10-12) has the most friction because it shifts from "creative planning" to "file creation" without sufficient formatting guidance or example references. A new user who ALSO browses the repo's existing files will succeed. A new user who ONLY reads the SOP will struggle at Step 10 and produce something that may not match the expected format.

**Time actually spent:** ~2 hours (within the SOP's 2.5-3.5 hour estimate, on the faster end because I had an AI helping).
